  • Gurol Suel

Gurol Suel


Associate Professor
Section of Molecular Biology, UCSD

e-mail: gsuel@ucsd.edu
Lab Homepage: Suel Lab

     Our laboratory utilizes systems and synthetic biology approaches to uncover fundamental principles underlying the heterogeneity in cellular decision-making at both the single cell and population levels. Specifically, we are interested in uncovering how molecular interactions comprising gene regulatory circuits can give rise to epigenetic heterogeneity. For this purpose, we integrate quantitative measurements and mathematical modeling across molecular, single cell and population scales. Our ultimate goal is to understand the evolutionary selection pressures that may have selected for specific cellular decision-making strategies at both the single cell and population levels.

     Dr. Süel received his PhD in Molecular Biophysics from the University of Texas, Southwestern Medical Center in the laboratory of Dr. Rama Ranganathan and conducted his postdoctoral studies in the laboratory of Dr Michael Elowitz in the Department of Biology and Applied Physics at the California Institute of Technology.
