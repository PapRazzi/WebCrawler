Question: What Is the AP Chemistry Exam?
High school students may choose to take the AP Chemistry course, which culminates in the AP Chemistry Exam. Here's a look at what the AP Chemistry Exam is and how it is graded.
Answer: The AP Chemistry Exam is test that evaluates your command of the material covered in a college chemistry course. This exam has two section and lasts three hours. Each section of the test counts for 50% of your grade. The first section consists of multiple choice questions. The second section consists of problems and short essays.

The AP Chemistry Exam is offered once a year in May. The test is scored in June, so you won't receive your grade immediately. A computer scores the multiple choice portion of the exam. A committee of high school and college teachers scores the problems and essays according to a set of standards.

The AP Chemistry Exam is graded on a curve. There is a five-point grade scale. 15% of test takers receive a 5 (best), 18% get a 4, 24% receive a 3, 18% get a 2, and 25% are given a 1 (no recommendation for college credit). Of the colleges that offer college credit for the AP exam, most award full credit for a 5, some allow credit for a 3 or 4, and you rarely get credit for a 2.

Students and their parents should understand that receiving college credit for a subject may not exempt you from having to take the class in college. College credit consists of hours of credit. Some colleges will require students to take general chemistry, especially if they are pursuing a degree in science.
