NYU-Poly Description:

The Polytechnic Institute of New York University, more commonly called NYU-Poly, is the new identity of a school that's been around for a while. Located in Brooklyn and founded in 1854, Polytechnic University merged with NYU in 2008 to form the new institute. The merger created a strong engineering research center and business incubator for the New York City region. NYU-Poly has a bit more of a graduate than undergraduate focus, but undergrads will find lots of opportunities to get their hands dirty working with faculty members and graduate students. The institute approaches academics with a model they call i2e -- invention, innovation, and entrepreneurship -- as students confront 21st century problems with an eye to both business and engineering challenges. On the athletic front, the NYU-Poly Fighting Blue Jays compete in the NCAA Division III Skyline Conference.
